Model for Prediction Across Scales-Ocean (MPAS-Ocean)

The Model for Prediction Across Scales, MPAS-Ocean, is an unstructured-mesh global ocean model designed for climate change research. It is a component of the Accelerated Climate Model for Energy (ACME), a new climate model sponsored by the Department of Energy. The ability to run high-resolution global simulations efficiently on large, high-performance computers is a priority for ACME. Model components, such as MPAS-Ocean, must be thoroughly validated in numerous settings, from idealized domains to real-world simulations. Model output is compared to the historical record of satellite and shipboard observations, as well as other ocean models. ACME includes active ocean, atmosphere, sea ice, land surface, and land ice components. Research goals with ACME include investigating the interaction between floating ice shelves, the sub-ice ocean, and the ice grounding line against bedrock. We are coupling MPAS-Ocean to the MPAS-Land Ice model to better understand how changing ocean temperature and currents influence glacial melting and retreat. These simulations take advantage of the horizontal variable-resolution mesh and vertically adaptive mesh in MPAS-Ocean.
